## Description

House music was born in Chicago in the early 1980s, pioneered by DJs at clubs like the Warehouse. It revolutionized electronic dance music with its repetitive 4/4 beats, synthesized basslines, and soulful vocals.

## Music

House music originated from disco, funk, and electronic music. DJs used drum machines, synthesizers, and samplers to create a new sound that defined club culture. The genre typically features 120-130 BPM with four-on-the-floor kick drums.

## Notable Figures

- Frankie Knuckles - 'The Godfather of House Music', DJ at the Warehouse
- Larry Heard (Mr. Fingers) - Pioneer of deep house
- Marshall Jefferson - Producer of 'Move Your Body', known as the house music anthem

## Description

Voguing is a highly stylized dance form that emerged from Harlem's Black and Latino LGBTQ+ ballroom culture in the 1980s. It features angular, geometric poses inspired by fashion models and Egyptian hieroglyphics, along with fluid movements and dramatic performances.

## Music

House music and ballroom beats, popularized internationally by Madonna's 'Vogue' (1990). The ballroom scene features performances to uptempo house and electronic music.

## Notable Figures

- Willi Ninja - Legendary voguer, 'Godfather of Voguing'
- Hector Xtravaganza - Founder of House of Xtravaganza
- Leiomy Maldonado - 'Wonder Woman of Vogue', modern icon

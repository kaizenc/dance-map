## Description

Litefeet (also known as 'getting lite') is a street dance form that emerged in Harlem and across New York City in the early 2000s. It features rapid footwork, spins, acrobatic moves, and hat tricks performed on subway platforms and street corners.

## Music

Uptempo hip-hop and R&B tracks, particularly those with Jersey Club and Baltimore Club influences. Dancers often perform to contemporary hip-hop with fast beats.

## Notable Figures

- Young B - Pioneer of the Litefeet movement
- Tone Doe - Influential Litefeet dancer
- Various subway and street performers throughout NYC
